	NAVY
	  

	Vigor Marine LLC, Portland, Oregon, is being awarded a $15,630,003 firm-fixed-price contract for a 75-calendar-day shipyard availability for the regular overhaul and dry docking of USNS Charles Drew (T-AKE 10). Work will include main diesel generator overhaul, life raft annual certification, drydocking, propeller shaft and stern inspection, underwater hull cleaning and painting, freeboard cleaning and painting, overhaul sea valves, five-year firefighting inspections, and renew flight deck non-skid. The contract includes options which, if exercised, would bring the total contract value to $15,776,416. Work will be performed in Portland, Oregon, and is expected to be completed by Sept. 11, 2015. Fiscal 2015 maintenance and repair funds in the amount of $15,630,003, excluding options, are obligated at time of award. This contract was competitively procured, with proposals solicited via the Federal Business Opportunities website and two offers received. The Navy’s Military Sealift Command is the contracting activity (N32205-15-C-3015).
	  

	BAE Systems San Francisco Ship Repair of San Francisco, California, is being awarded a $13,592,510 firm-fixed-price contract for a 45-calendar-day shipyard availability for the regular overhaul and dry docking of the USNS Rainier (T-AOE 7). Work will include elevator fork truck guard deck and sump removal, upgrade FAS station oil containment (T-ALT 204), thrust bearing inspection, switchboard and load center cleaning, INMARSAT B preventive maintenance, gallery/scullery equipment maintenance, overhaul miscellaneous fans and motors, dry-docking and undocking vessel, steel renewal, main propulsion shafting, and rudders and stern frame examination. The contract includes options which, if exercised, would bring the total contract value to $15,435,092. Work will be performed in San Francisco, California, and is expected to be completed by Aug. 27, 2015. Fiscal 2015 maintenance and repair funds in the amount of $13,592,510, excluding options, are being obligated at time of award. This contract was competitively procured, with proposals solicited via the Federal Business Opportunities website and one offer received. The Navy’s Military Sealift Command is the contracting activity (N32205-15-C-2005). 

	
	BAE Systems, Greenlawn, New York, is being awarded a $10,850,416 firm-fixed-price contract for the software updates to implement the Radio Technical Commission for Aeronautics DO-260B-compliant Automatic Dependent Surveillance-Broadcast capability into the RT-1763C/APX-111(V) combined interrogator-transponder in support of the F/A-18 and EA-18G aircraft. Work will be performed in Greenlawn, New York, and is expected to be completed in January 2018. Fiscal 2015 aircraft procurement funds (Navy) in the amount of $10,850,416 are being obligated at time of award, none of which will expire at the end of the current fiscal year. This contract was not competitively procured pursuant to Federal Acquisition Regulation 6.302-1. The Naval Air Systems Command, Patuxent River, Maryland, is the contracting activity (N00019-15-C-3001). 

	
	Raytheon Co., McKinney, Texas, is being awarded $10,405,640 for firm-fixed-price delivery order 7041 under a previously awarded basic ordering agreement (N00383-10-G-006D) for repair of units in support of the H-60 multi-spectral targeting system forward looking infrared system. Work will be performed in Jacksonville, Florida, and is expected to be completed by June 2017. Fiscal 2015 working capital (Navy) funds in the amount of $10,405,640 will be obligated at time of award, none of which will expire at the end of the current fiscal year. 	Alliant Techsystems Operations LLC, Northridge, California, is being awarded a $7,481,806 cost-plus-fixed-fee, indefinite-delivery/indefinite-quantity contract for the design, development and studies, and technology demonstrations required to develop and apply new technology to ensure battlespace dominance. 	AIR FORCE
	  

	GE Aviation, Cincinnati, Ohio, has been awarded an $11,999,218 firm-fixed-priced order (0008) from the basic ordering agreement FA8122-14-G-0001 for F110 Service Life Extension Program (SLEP) kits. Contractor will provide six SLEP kits composed of 17 individual kits that support the F110-100 engines used in the F-16 fleet. Work will be performed at Cincinnati, Ohio, and is expected to be complete by June 30, 2015. This contract involves foreign military sales to Egypt. This award is the result of a sole-source acquisition. Air Force Material Command, Tinker Air Force Base, Oklahoma, is the contracting activity.
